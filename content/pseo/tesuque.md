# Tesuque

**H1:** Tesuque Wildfire Defense: Spanish Goats for Riverside Vegetation Management

**Meta Description:** Tesuque's river valley vegetation creates unique fire risks. Professional goat grazing reduces fuel loads while protecting your property's natural beauty.

**Intro Paragraph:**
Tesuque's cottonwood bosque and riverside vegetation present fire management challenges unlike anywhere else in Northern New Mexico. The Tesuque River corridor creates microclimates where Russian olive, salt cedar, and native grasses grow thick—beautiful for wildlife, dangerous during fire season. Historic properties along the Tesuque Valley face additional pressure: maintaining the area's rural character while meeting modern defensible space requirements. Traditional brush clearing destroys the landscape's natural beauty, but targeted goat grazing preserves mature trees while systematically removing understory fire fuels. Our Spanish goats navigate Tesuque's arroyos and irrigation ditches with precision, creating graduated fuel breaks that complement the valley's agricultural heritage. Great Pyrenees guardian dogs ensure controlled grazing that respects both your property boundaries and neighboring equestrian operations.
