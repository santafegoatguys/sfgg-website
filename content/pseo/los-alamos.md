# Los Alamos

**H1:** Los Alamos Fire Prevention: Precision Goat Grazing for Mesa Communities

**Meta Description:** Los Alamos mesa terrain demands specialized fire prevention. SFGG's targeted grazing creates defensible space in challenging topography.

**Intro Paragraph:**
Los Alamos' mesa-top location and steep canyon access create unique wildfire evacuation challenges that make defensible space absolutely critical. The 2011 Las Conchas Fire proved how quickly flames can race up canyon walls toward residential areas. Los Alamos homes often back to undeveloped USFS land thick with Gambel oak, serviceberry, and mountain mahogany—native species that become fire ladders during dry conditions. Conventional brush clearing on mesa edges risks erosion, but Spanish goats excel on steep terrain, creating natural firebreaks while preserving soil stability. Our precision grazing approach works systematically through Los Alamos' characteristic vegetation, reducing fuel loads to meet NFPA 1144 standards while maintaining the area's forested character. Great Pyrenees guardian dogs provide security in areas where wildlife encounters are common.
