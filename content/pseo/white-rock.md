# White Rock

**H1:** White Rock Defensible Space: Goat Grazing for Canyon-Edge Properties

**Meta Description:** White Rock's canyon-edge homes need specialized fire defense. Professional goat grazing creates defensible space on challenging terrain safely.

**Intro Paragraph:**
White Rock's dramatic canyon-edge setting offers stunning views and serious fire risks. Perched above the Rio Grande, many homes feature native landscaping with Apache plume, rabbit brush, and cholla that becomes dangerous during fire season. The community's history with the 2000 Cerro Grande Fire underscores why proactive vegetation management matters—evacuation options are limited when flames approach from multiple directions. Traditional brush clearing on White Rock's slopes risks destabilizing canyon-edge properties, but targeted goat grazing works with the terrain's natural contours. Spanish goats systematically browse fire-prone vegetation while their hooves naturally create firebreaks along property lines. Our Great Pyrenees guardian dogs ensure controlled grazing that respects White Rock's wildlife corridors and neighboring open space areas.
