# Eldorado

**H1:** Eldorado Fire Risk Management: Targeted Goat Grazing for Defensible Space

**Meta Description:** Eldorado's piñon-juniper terrain needs proactive fire defense. SFGG's targeted goat grazing creates defensible space meeting NM EMNRD standards.

**Intro Paragraph:**
Eldorado's rolling hills and piñon-juniper woodlands create the perfect storm for wildfire risk. With homes nestled among native vegetation and limited escape routes along Highway 285, defensible space isn't optional—it's essential. The 2022 Hermits Peak/Calf Canyon Fire reminded all of Northern New Mexico that fire season arrives earlier and burns hotter each year. Eldorado's proximity to the Galisteo Basin means homes face unique challenges: dense understory growth, cholla patches, and four-wing saltbush that conventional clearing methods struggle to manage effectively. Santa Fe Goat Guys' Spanish goat herds excel in this terrain, systematically reducing fuel loads while creating the 30-foot defensible space zones required by New Mexico EMNRD standards. Our livestock guardian dogs ensure safe, controlled grazing that transforms fire-prone landscapes into community assets.
