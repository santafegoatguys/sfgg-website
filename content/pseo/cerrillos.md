# Cerrillos

**H1:** Cerrillos Fire Prevention: Mining District Vegetation Management Solutions

**Meta Description:** Cerrillos' mining heritage terrain creates complex fire risks. Professional goat grazing navigates historic sites while reducing fuel loads.

**Intro Paragraph:**
Cerrillos' mining district landscape presents fire management challenges found nowhere else in Northern New Mexico. Historic mine sites, slag piles, and reclaimed areas support unique vegetation communities that become fire hazards during dry seasons. The community's location between the Ortiz and San Pedro mountains means homes face fire risks from multiple directions, while narrow roads limit evacuation options. Properties often feature native landscaping mixed with volunteer vegetation on disturbed soils—four-wing saltbush, chamisa, and pioneer species that cure quickly and burn intensely. Traditional clearing methods struggle with Cerrillos' rocky terrain and historic site restrictions, but Spanish goats navigate these conditions naturally. Our targeted grazing approach works systematically through complex topography, creating defensible space that complements the area's mining heritage while meeting current fire safety standards.
