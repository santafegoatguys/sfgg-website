# Edgewood

**H1:** Edgewood Fire Risk Reduction: Targeted Grazing for High Desert Homes

**Meta Description:** Edgewood's high desert vegetation creates year-round fire risks. Spanish goat grazing reduces fuel loads while preserving native plant communities.

**Intro Paragraph:**
Edgewood's high desert environment along the eastern edge of the Sandia Mountains creates fire conditions that can ignite any season. Unlike higher elevation communities, Edgewood's piñon-juniper woodland mixed with grasslands means fine fuels cure early and stay flammable longer. Properties along Highway 344 and throughout the Edgewood area face challenges from dense stands of one-seed juniper, four-wing saltbush, and invasive species that create continuous fuel beds. The community's rapid growth means newer homes often lack established defensible space, while older properties struggle with overgrown native vegetation. Santa Fe Goat Guys' Spanish goat herds thrive in these high desert conditions, systematically reducing fuel loads while promoting healthy native plant regeneration. Our targeted grazing creates the graduated defensible space zones that give Edgewood families crucial time during wildfire events.
