## Core Questions — In Order

### 1. Distribution list / guidelines timeline
> "When are the FY27 Wildfire Prepared Program guidelines expected to publish, and how do we get on the distribution list to receive them the day they drop?"

*Listen for: exact date, email list signup, state procurement portal notification, or a contact name.*

---

### 2. Qualified-entity path (most important)
> "Section 4.A.2 of SB 33 references 'qualified entities' assisting eligible property owners with mitigation work. What is the process for a private contractor to be recognized as a qualified entity under the program — is that a registration, an RFP, a state vendor list, or something else?"

*Listen for: RFP process, SHARE procurement registration, a pre-qualified vendor list, or a grant sub-award process through counties/municipalities.*

*Follow-up if they say counties administer it:*
> "Got it — so the path is to get named in a county application rather than apply directly to EMNRD? And the county can name us as their execution contractor?"

---

### 3. Vendor/RFP calendar
> "Is there an RFP calendar for Wildfire Prepared Program contracts, or does that procurement happen through local governments — counties, municipalities, Fireshed Coalitions?"

*Listen for: state-level RFPs, pass-through to counties, or no formal procurement (just county discretion).*

---

### 4. SHARE registration
> "If contractors need to be registered in SHARE to receive state-funded payments, do you know if that requirement flows down to subcontractors under a county sub-grant, or only to direct EMNRD vendors?"

*This tells you whether Carl needs to register in SHARE now or whether the county absorbs that requirement.*

---

### 5. IBHS / assessor certification
> "The statute names IBHS Wildfire Prepared Home as the assessment standard. We have a team member pursuing IBHS assessor certification right now. Is EMNRD treating IBHS certification as a requirement for assessors under Section 4.A.1.c, or a preferred credential?"

*Listen for: hard requirement vs. soft preference; whether they're tracking certified assessors.*
